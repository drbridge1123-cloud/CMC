# CMC Case Lifecycle Pipeline

## Overview

This document describes the full case lifecycle from initial client contact to commission payout. Each stage corresponds to a page/module in the CMC system.

---

## Pipeline Stages

### 1. Referrals (Intake)

- **Who**: Jimi (primary intake staff)
- **What**: New leads come in via phone, referral sources, or other channels
- **Actions**:
  - Enter client information (name, contact, accident details)
  - Assign lead to appropriate staff member
  - Initial consultation and case evaluation
- **System Page**: Referrals
- **Next**: Case Tracker

---

### 2. Case Tracker (Case Creation)

- **Who**: Assigned case manager / staff
- **What**: A formal case is created from the referral
- **Actions**:
  - Case file created with full client details
  - Staff assigned (case manager, attorney, etc.)
  - Case status tracking begins
  - Documents and communication logged
- **System Page**: Case Tracker
- **Next**: Prelitigation

---

### 3. Prelitigation (Treatment Phase)

- **Who**: Assigned prelitigation staff (Dave, Soyong, Chloe, Jimi, Daniel)
- **What**: Client undergoes medical treatment for their injuries
- **Actions**:
  - Coordinate medical treatment with providers
  - Track treatment progress and appointments
  - Monitor treatment timeline
  - Collect treatment records as they come in
  - Mark treatment as complete when done
- **System Page**: Prelitigation Tracker
- **Next**: Billing Tracker (1st pass)

---

### 4. Billing Tracker — 1st Pass (Records & Billing Collection)

- **Who**: Billing staff
- **What**: Collect all medical records and billing documents after treatment completes
- **Actions**:
  - Request medical records from all providers
  - Request billing statements from all providers
  - Track which records/bills have been received
  - Compile complete medical billing package
  - Verify all documentation is complete
- **System Page**: Billing Tracker
- **Next**: Demand

---

### 5. Demand (Demand Letter)

- **Who**: Attorney / case manager
- **What**: Send a demand letter to the insurance company
- **Actions**:
  - Prepare demand package (medical records, bills, demand letter)
  - Calculate total damages and demand amount
  - Send demand to insurance company
  - Track demand status and response
- **System Page**: Demand
- **Next**: Attorney Tracker

---

### 6. Attorney Tracker (Negotiation & Settlement)

- **Who**: Attorney
- **What**: Negotiate with insurance company toward settlement
- **Actions**:
  - Review insurance company response to demand
  - Negotiate settlement amount
  - Handle litigation if necessary
  - Reach settlement agreement
  - Process settlement documents
- **System Page**: Attorney Tracker
- **Next**: Billing Tracker (2nd pass)

---

### 7. Billing Tracker — 2nd Pass (Final Balance Checkup)

- **Who**: Billing staff
- **What**: After settlement, verify all billing is accurate and finalized
- **Actions**:
  - Final balance checkup on all medical bills
  - Negotiate any outstanding balances with providers
  - Confirm final amounts owed to each provider
  - Prepare final billing summary for accounting
- **System Page**: Billing Tracker
- **Next**: Accounting

---

### 8. Accounting (Financial Processing)

- **Who**: Accounting staff
- **What**: Process the financial distribution of the settlement
- **Actions**:
  - Receive finalized billing from Billing Tracker
  - Calculate distribution (attorney fees, medical liens, client portion)
  - Process payments to providers
  - Process client disbursement
  - Financial record keeping
- **System Page**: Accounting
- **Next**: Commissions

---

### 9. Commissions (Staff Payouts)

- **Who**: Management / Accounting
- **What**: Calculate and pay staff commissions on the settled case
- **Actions**:
  - Calculate commission based on settlement and staff roles
  - Process commission payouts
  - Record commission payments
- **System Page**: Commissions
- **End of pipeline**

---

## Separate Track: Traffic Cases

Traffic cases follow their own workflow outside the main pipeline. These are handled independently and do not flow through the standard case lifecycle stages above.

- **System Page**: Traffic Cases
